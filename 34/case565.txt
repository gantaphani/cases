in a split decision,a jury acquitted anthony gobbi on three counts of a indictment but convicted him of conspiracy and one substantive charge.on appeal,gobbi mounts a attack,challenging i the sufficiency of the evidence underpinning one of the two counts of conviction ii an evidentiary ruling and iii two sentencing enhancements.concluding,as we do,that gobbi arguments are devoid of merit,we affirm the judgment below.
background 
this case grows out of a reverse sting operation conceived by the federal bureau of investigation fbi.we rehearse the relevant facts in the light most hospitable to the jury verdict on the counts of conviction,consistent with record support.see united states,cir.
on june,an fbi agent,michael mcgowan,posing as a wealthy businessman,met with robert nardolillo a suspected underworld figure.mcgowan said that his business partner manny wanted to enter the new england drug trade and could supply nardolillo with copious amounts of drugs.nardolillo expressed interest and acknowledged that he could handle two to three kilograms of cocaine per week,and possibly more.the men agreed to meet again so that mcgowan could introduce his apocryphal partner to nardolillo.
that fall,nardolillo and gobbi traveled to lauderdale,florida.they met mcgowan there on september.after mcgowan explained that manny was unavoidably detained,gobbi left.mcgowan and nardolillo nonetheless proceeded to discuss the planned venture.
thereafter,mcgowan and nardolillo spoke regularly by telephone about the prospects of doing business together.nardolillo instructed mcgowan to contact him through his associates including gobbi,and gobbi in fact acted as an intermediary for sixteen of the seventeen contacts between mcgowan and nardolillo in january of.
on january,nardolillo and gobbi finally came face to face with manny in reality,another undercover fbi agent.this meeting took place at centerfolds,a providence strip club.following the initial introduction,nardolillo spoke privately with manny for about an hour.
on january,mcgowan approached nardolillo about a possible detail.the idea was that nardolillo and his crew would guard a cache of cocaine temporarily located in rhode island en route to distribution throughout new england.nardolillo readily agreed.mcgowan and nardolillo settled on remuneration at the rate of per kilogram for the protective detail.on march,nardolillo assured mcgowan that i put my guys there,do worry.there be somebody there to watch manny stuff all the time.switching to a different topic,mcgowan informed nardolillo that manny did not yet have five kilograms of cocaine that nardolillo had expressed interest in buying for his own distribution network.
on april,mcgowan,nardolillo,and gobbi met at a funeral.because law enforcement officers were surveilling the scene,gobbi escorted mcgowan into an office so that he mcgowan could confer privately with nardolillo about the particulars of the detail.later that month,nardolillo told mcgowan that he would assign two guys to the detail that both would be that the man directly guarding the drug cache would be prepared to kill if necessary and that he nardolillo would divide the agreed payment between the two operatives.mcgowan informed nardolillo that manny wanted a gun displayed for a show of force,but that it was not to be fired.
the detail was scheduled for april.according to clifford falla,gobbi recruited him for this job.on the assigned date,gobbi and falla met with nardolillo at centerfolds.nardolillo told the pair that he had arranged for a detail at a local hotel and that falla was to guard the drugs.in gobbi presence,nardolillo gave falla a pistol and directed him to display it while on guard.nardolillo explained that gobbi would lead falla to the hotel and wait outside.

ii.discussion 
on appeal,gobbi advances three assignments of error.these relate,respectively,to the sufficiency of the government proof,an evidentiary ruling,and the sentence imposed.we consider each claim in turn.
sufficiency of the evidence.
does not challenge the sufficiency of the evidence underlying his conspiracy conviction count maintains that the proof adduced at trial did not permit his conviction on count.he argues,in effect,that while there may have been evidence of an overall intent to distribute,there was no evidence to establish that any of the three named defendants ever attempted to possess the cocaine.he emphasizes that there was no evidence a that he,personally,had an intent to possess cocaine or that he took a substantial step toward that end b that either of his codefendants had such an intent or c that the cocaine was destined for any of the three men.
we review preserved objections to evidentiary sufficiency de novo.see,united states hern√°ndez,cir.under that standard of review,we will affirm a conviction as long as the total evidence,with all reasonable inferences made in the light most favorable to the government,is such that a rational trier of fact could have found guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.united states loder,cir.in this undertaking,we consider both direct and circumstantial evidence,upholding the jury verdict when it is supported by a plausible rendition of the record.united states ortiz,cir.
here,however,gobbi made no timely motion for judgment of acquittal below.see.in the absence of such a motion,we can provide relief on a sufficiency challenge only if the verdict threatens to work a clear and gross injustice.see,at united states hadfield,cir.as we explain below,the evidence in this case suffices to sustain the conviction under the ordinary standard of review and,a fortiori,the conviction survives review under the latter standard.
with respect to count,the district court instructed the jury,without objection,that it could find gobbi guilty based on any of three theories a standard attempt formulation,aiding and abetting,or pinkerton liability the jury returned a general verdict on count.the law is crystalline that,when the government has advanced several alternate theories of guilt and the trial court has submitted the case to the jury on that basis,an ensuing conviction may stand as long as the evidence suffices to support any one of the submitted theories.see,griffin united states,d united states moran,cir.here,the aiding and abetting theory appears to be the government strongest,so we start there.
to prove attempt,the government must show that a defendant intended to commit the substantive offense and that he took a substantial step toward its commission.united states doyon,cir.here,the substantive of drugs with intent to criminalized by a.for purposes of that statute,possession may be either actual or constructive.see united states bergodere,cir.actual possession is the state of immediate,physical possession.united states zavala maldonado,cir.constructive possession occurs when a person knowingly has the power and intention at a given time to exercise dominion and control over an object,either directly or through others.united states,cir citation and internal quotation marks omitted.constructive possession of drugs may be inferred from a defendant dominion and control over an area where narcotics are found.united states echeverri,cir.
aided by these abstract principles,we must determine whether a reasonable jury could have found that gobbi aided and abetted an attempt to possess cocaine on april,the distribution element is obvious,and so it need not be examined.since there is no suggestion of actual possession,the question reduces to one of constructive possession.this question demands an affirmative answer the evidence,taken in the light most favorable to the verdict,supports a conclusion that one of gobbi accomplices,falla,intended constructively to possess the ersatz cocaine handled during the april detail that he took substantial steps in that direction and that gobbi aided and abetted him in that endeavor.we elaborate below.
on this record,the jury reasonably could have found that falla understood the purpose of a detail that he took a handgun to the hotel room in order to advance that goal that he displayed the weapon to establish his dominion and control over the cocaine that no one else in the room made a similar show of force and that he remained in the room until the job ended.although falla did not have exclusive possession of the cocaine,the government did not need to prove that he did culpable possession,whether actual or constructive,may be either exclusive or joint.zavala maldonado,at.falla could be found to have had joint constructive possession of the cocaine,and to have intended to exclude others,not in the room,from possessing it.no more was exigible.
of course,there was no cocaine only a substance that resembled cocaine,so falla could be found guilty only of attempted possession.see supra note.the question,then,is whether the jury reasonably could have found that gobbi aided and abetted falla in this attempted possession.we think that it could have done so.
a defendant may be convicted of aiding and abetting when the evidence shows that the underlying offense was committed by a principal that the defendant consciously shared the principal knowledge and that he willfully associated himself in some way with the crime and willfully participated in it as he would in something he wished to bring about.united states keene,cir citation and internal quotation marks omitted.viewing falla as the principal,the first prong of this paradigm was satisfied.so,too,was the second the jury reasonably could have found that gobbi consciously shared falla guilty knowledge.after all,there was evidence that gobbi recruited falla for the venture and stood by while nardolillo armed him and described how the job was to be done.
the third part of the paradigm was also satisfied.the record readily supports a finding of gobbi culpable participation.to mention only a few items,there was evidence that gobbi led falla to the hotel,provided the keycard to the room,kept watch from the street,relayed information to and from nardolillo during the course of the detail,and spoke several times with falla in an effort to ensure the successful completion of the operation.
the short of it is that the evidence was ample to ground a finding of guilt on an aiding and abetting theory.see,united states harris,cir.gobbi,however,endeavors to blunt the force of this reasoning,citing a covey of inapposite cases.see,united states yossunthorn,cir united states harris,cir.he also seeks to exploit a perceived discrepancy in the government proof.we briefly discuss that discrepancy.
a video surveillance record introduced into evidence indicated that falla entered the hotel room at around twilight on april.a law enforcement officer testified,however,that gobbi left the gas station before dark.gobbi strives to convince us that this seeming inconsistency is of great moment.we are not persuaded.
taking a narrow view of this supposed discrepancy,gobbi argument is undermined by points he himself raises the officer testimony that he first noticed gobbi car at the gas station after falla had entered the hotel,and falla testimony that he had a conversation with gobbi in the hotel parking lot just before entering the hotel.the testimony is not perfectly consistent but,taking a more global view,we are constrained to note that trial testimony often is a notoriously imperfect means of reproducing past events.exactitude is desirable,but it is not a necessary condition to a finding of credibility.minor inconsistencies are common,unavoidable,and virtually always grist for the factfinder mill.it was ultimately the province of the jury to assess the significance of any contradictions in the a contradiction as petty as this one.see united states thomas,cir.
we have said enough on this score.having reviewed the record with care,we conclude that the evidence is sufficient to support gobbi conviction on count of the indictment on an aiding and abetting theory.that conclusion,in terms,renders it unnecessary for us to consider either of the government alternate theories of guilt.
admission of evidence.
gobbi next claim of error is that the district court improperly admitted evidence of discussions about a possible drug sale from manny to nardolillo.the main thrust of this claim is that the conspiracy charge count centered around a detail rather than a scheme and that,therefore,the challenged conversations which spanned a period from june of through january of were unfairly prejudicial,and unjustifiably injected evidence of other uncharged bad acts into the case.
we set the stage.on the first day of trial gobbi,relying on evidence rule b and arguably on evidence rule purposes of this appeal,we give gobbi the benefit of the to evidence of a january conversation relating to the phase of the investigation.the district court overruled the objection.the court later extended its ruling to cover this entire line of testimony.
the two rules upon which gobbi relies are familiar.the former rule prohibits evidence of other uncharged crimes or bad acts to prove the character of a person in order to show action in conformity therewith.b.the latter rule provides for the exclusion of relevant evidence if its probative value is substantially outweighed by,inter alia,a danger of unfair prejudice.we examine the force of each rule separately,mindful that an appellate court customarily reviews a trial court rulings admitting or excluding evidence only for abuse of discretion.see,at.
rule b.the prohibition against other acts evidence typically refers to evidence that is extrinsic to the crime charged and introduced for the purpose of showing propensity.see,united states epstein,cir united states,cir.here,however,we never reach the question of propensity the evidence in this case is intrinsic to the conspiracy described in count of the indictment.that is to say,the evidence comprises part and parcel of the charged offense.thus,the evidence is not other acts evidence at all and,accordingly,rule b is not implicated.see united states,cir.
to be sure,gobbi calls attention to the prosecutor closing argument which focused narrowly on the two details and the fact that mcgowan did not have a single substantive conversation with gobbi about possible activity.on this basis,gobbi suggests that the conversations about drug purchases do not relate in any way to the conspiracy with which he was charged and of which he was convicted this is sheer persiflage.
the short answer to gobbi plaint is that the charged conspiracy was broader than he acknowledges.while there is no doubt that the lion share of the government proof on count centered around the phase of the conspiracy,the government also proffered evidence of an integrated phase.having knowingly joined the overall conspiracy,gobbi was criminally responsible for both phases of its illicit endeavors.united states barnes,cir after all,a conspiracy is a continuum.once a participant knowingly helps to initiate the agreement and set it in motion,he assumes conspirator responsibility for the foreseeable actions of his confederates within the scope of the conspiratorial agreement.
as described in the indictment,the charged conspiracy spanned a period of approximately fourteen months,from june,through august.several of the discussions pertained to the phase,and the record supports a finding that gobbi knew of the full sweep of the conspiracy given this configuration,as a whole,we can not say that the district court erred in determining that evidence of nardolillo interest in purchasing a supply of cocaine from manny and distributing those drugs locally with the help of his crew was part of the warp and woof of the conspiracy charged.see,at united states jesus,cir.
rule.gobbi claim of prejudice fares no better.although the conversations spanned a period of many months,the testimony about them was relatively brief and its effect was more likely to cast nardolillo,rather than gobbi,in a villainous light.
this does not mean,of course,that there was absolutely no prejudice.by design,all evidence is meant to be prejudicial it is only unfair prejudice which must be avoided,at.the trial judge,who saw and heard the evidence play out,apparently discerned no unfair prejudice here.that implicit determination was well within the realm of his considerable discretion.see id.at only in extraordinarily compelling we,from the vista of a cold appellate record,reverse a district court judgment concerning the relative weighing of probative value and unfair effect.quoting freeman package mach,cir.
