petitioner henry mboowa,a native and citizen of uganda,asks us to review a board of immigration appeals bia order denying his claims for asylum,withholding of removal,and protection under the united nations convention against torture cat.the bia upheld an immigration judge ij finding that mboowa testimony was not credible and,thus,that he was unable to establish eligibility for relief.after careful review,however,we conclude that the record does not support two of the purported discrepancies that the agency considered critical in discrediting mboowa account.in light of that finding,we grant mboowa petition,vacate the bia order,and remand for additional proceedings 
mboowa was born in kampala,uganda in.he entered the united states through newark,new jersey on june,to work as a summer camp counselor as part of an exchange program.although his j visa authorized only a temporary stay until september,he has remained in the united states without authorization ever since.
on february,mboowa applied pro se for asylum but an asylum officer denied his application.no further action was taken until february,when the department of homeland security served mboowa with a notice to appear in immigration removal proceedings.mboowa appeared with legal counsel at an initial hearing on july,and conceded that the notice to appear factual allegations were accurate.nevertheless,mboowa indicated that he would seek asylum,withholding of removal,and protection under cat,and he proffered his original,asylum application as support for his claims.
beyond his asylum application,mboowa has recounted the events underlying his claims on several occasions.at the initial hearing on july,in addition to submitting his original application,he provided several documents purporting to corroborate his recollection of events and describing the political conditions in uganda.mboowa also filed additional corroborating documents and an affidavit on june.mboowa then testified before the ij on december,and once again filed additional supporting documentation.
the political situation in uganda,where incumbent president yoweri museveni has remained in office since,supplies the backdrop for mboowa claims.mboowa alleges that he joined a youth pressure group called the youth unity peace initiative yupi in.although initially focused on certain policy issues,the group become directly involved in electoral politics as the ugandan presidential election approached.yupi ultimately supported the opposition candidate,colonel besigye,whom museveni would defeat in march.mboowa alleges that his membership in yupi and the group support of besigye left mboowa and his family vulnerable to persecution by those loyal to president museveni,including the ugandan military and intelligence services.
mboowa claims rest primarily on four incidents that took place between and a beating,a home invasion,the death of mboowa father,and the beheading of mboowa cousin.we briefly describe the facts central to those events according to mboowa testimony although,as discussed later,the agency identified certain purported inconsistencies in mboowa accounts.
on january,mboowa and a yupi colleague,moses sekibuule,were accosted and beaten in kampala.mboowa alleges that,while the two men were hanging campaign posters supporting colonel besigye,more than a dozen soldiers dressed in camouflage suddenly approached in a truck.they demanded that mboowa and sekibuule cease hanging the posters and ordered the two men to lie on the ground.the soldiers proceeded to whip,kick,and beat the two men for twelve to fifteen minutes.as a result of the beating mboowa maintains that his injuries a broken pelvis,deep wounds on his shins,cuts to his knees,and several cuts and a deep gash along the side of his head required a hospital stay.sekibuule allegedly lost several teeth and sustained either a broken hand or a broken arm.
a second incident followed on february.mboowa recalls that while he was sleeping several armed men broke into his residence,blindfolded him,ransacked his house,vandalized his property,and struck him on the jaw with the butt of a gun.before departing,the men allegedly stole several yupi files and mboowa membership card.mboowa testified that he gathered the men were from the military intelligence agency because they warned him that this was the price to pay for not supporting the incumbent president.
the following month,president museveni defeated colonel besigye in the presidential election.according to mboowa,however,the consequences of supporting besigye did not end with museveni successful reelection.one such consequence,mboowa alleges,was the mysterious death of his father in march.his father also had been politically active and,at the time of his death,was the mobilizing secretary for a second group,reform agenda,which also had supported colonel besigye.mboowa asserts that,after not hearing from his father for several days,his family suspected that his father was detained although he does not specify by whom.at some point,a local dispensary contacted the family and informed them that it was providing care for a man identifying himself as mboowa father.upon finding his father malnourished and in poor condition,mboowa moved him to a hospital.by the time they arrived at the hospital,mboowa father tongue had turned entirely black,and his body was cold and sweating.although the medical staff did not know exactly what was wrong,mboowa testified that the hospital concluded his father had signs of poisoning.his father subsequently died during surgery.
finally,mboowa testified that his cousin,also an active member of yupi,disappeared in april along with another colleague.the family lost contact with the cousin for a number of days but,upon reading a newspaper account of a man who had been shot,mboowa testified that the family started piecing things together.the family later learned that mboowa cousin and his colleague had been beheaded,and when mboowa aunts went to identify the bodies,they could only do so based on a distinctive birthmark on his cousin body.this act of violence was the last straw for mboowa,prompting him to leave uganda until the dust settles.
primarily on the basis of these four incidents mboowa testified that he fears he would be detained or killed if forced to return to uganda.the ij determined that the acts of harm described are plausible in light of country conditions,and would rise to the level of past persecution if established.but the ij deemed mboowa not credible and was thus unable to make a finding that these events actually occurred as described.after cataloging numerous internal inconsistencies and inconsistencies between his asylum application,affidavits and testimony and supporting documentation,the ij discredited mboowa testimony in its entirety.m ost troubling to the ij was that significant portions of mboowa testimony,especially his broken pelvis,three week hospital stay,and the beheading of his cousin were omitted from his original application,written only about a year after the events.without such evidence,the ij concluded mboowa had not demonstrated past persecution on account of a statutorily protected ground.the adverse credibility finding likewise doomed mboowa requests for asylum based on the fear of future persecution,withholding of removal,and protection under cat.
the bia affirmed,finding that the discrepancies identified are present and provide specific cogent reasons for the ij adverse credibility determination.the bia primarily recounted the inconsistencies surrounding mboowa description of the beating,and faulted mboowa for failing to mention in his supplemental filed as part of his initial asylum application that he was beaten with the guns of the soldiers but not whipped,that he sustained broken ribs and a broken hip,or that he was hospitalized for weeks.the bia concluded that mboowa failed to adequately explain these discrepancies as well as the other discrepancies that the immigration judge identified.the bia also remarked that several material inconsistencies that we do not mention here provide further support for the ij adverse credibility determination,and that mboowa failed to rehabilitate his credibility with corroborating evidence.this petition timely followed.
mboowa challenges the adverse credibility determination and resulting denial of his asylum,withholding of removal,and cat claims.to qualify for asylum,an applicant must establish that he has a fear of persecution on account of race,religion,nationality,membership in a particular social group,or political opinion.jin lin holder,cir quoting a a.the testimony of the applicant,alone,can suffice to meet this burden id.but where the agency determines that the testimony is not credible,that testimony may be discounted or completely disregarded.id.where,as here,the bia has written separately while deferring to and affirming the decision of an ij,we review both the bia decision and the relevant portions of the ij decision.lutaaya mukasey,cir.
we review the bia and ij credibility determination under the deferential substantial evidence standard.dhima gonzales,cir.that standard requires us to uphold the ruling unless the record would compel a reasonable adjudicator to reach a contrary determination.jin lin,at.our deference to the agency determination is not unbounded,however.the ij must provide a specific,cogent,and supportable explanation for rejecting an alien testimony.abdelmalek mukasey,cir quoting teng mukasey,cir.under the heart of the matter rule,the discrepancies relied upon to support an adverse credibility determination must pertain to facts central to the merits of the alien claims.zheng gonzales,cir.discrepancies that relate merely to peripheral or trivial matters are insufficient,alone,to support an adverse credibility determination.id.
accordingly,we are compelled to uphold the ij and bia adverse credibility determination in this case only if 
the discrepancies and omissions underlying the determination are actually present in the record those discrepancies and omissions provide specific and cogent reasons to conclude that the petitioner testimony was incredible with regard to facts central to the merits of his asylum claim and the petitioner has failed to provide a convincing explanation for the discrepancies and omissions.
jin lin,at.
the bia and ij opinions here fail on the first prong of this inquiry.after detailing a litany of purported inconsistencies,the ij found most troubling the omission of significant portions of mboowa testimony,especially his broken pelvis,three week hospital stay,and the beheading of his cousin,from his original application,written only about a year after the events.and the bia subsequently adopted that credibility determination.the ij recounting of the record evidence is only partially accurate,however.after a careful review,we conclude that although the ij classified mboowa allegations of a broken pelvis and the beheading of his cousin as purported omissions,both claims were consistently present in the record.
what the ij referred to generally as mboowa initial application in fact consists of two documents that were filed concurrently.the first is mboowa i form for asylum.entitled application for asylum and for withholding of removal,the i form is a government application form that asks a series of specific questions and provides limited space in which the alien may write or type an answer.mboowa form contains handwritten answers to the various questions.the i form also instructs an applicant to attach additional sheets of paper as needed to complete the applicant responses.mboowa did so here.he supplied a typed statement accompanying his i form that provides further detail regarding some of his claims.in our view,the i form and the supplemental statement are best viewed as a collective whole.
with that understanding in mind,we proceed to the inconsistencies the ij purported to identify.the ij is correct that one portion of mboowa initial application the supplemental statement discusses neither his pelvic injury nor his hospital stay and makes only passing reference to los ing a cousin after being trailed by the military.but the attached i form does disclose his hip injury and describes,in some detail,his cousin beheading.on page of the i form,in response to a question asking whether you,your family,or close friends or colleagues have ever experienced harm or mistreatment or threats in the past by anyone,mboowa hand wrote that he sustained cuts on the head and a broken hip during the beating.on the following page,in response to a question asking whether any family members had been accused,charged,arrested,detained,interrogated,convicted and sentenced,or imprisoned in any country other than the united states,mboowa hand wrote on april,my cousin and his colleagues were detained at an undisclosed location.they were interrogated and tortured,in their own country,without notifying any relatives.their bodies only surfaced three days later,with two of them beheaded.
in concluding that these allegations were absent from mboowa initial application,the agency appears to have placed talismanic weight on mboowa supplemental statement to the exclusion of the i form that statement accompanied.but the agency supplied no rationale justifying its focus on the supplemental statement,leaving us with a firm sense that the i form itself was overlooked.
with these allegations as contained in mboowa i form in mind,it is not accurate to characterize mboowa claims of a hip injury and his cousin beheading as fabrication or embellishment when those significant details are contained in one portion of mboowa initial application the filing that,as the ij pointed out,set forth mboowa account closest in time to the underlying events themselves.to be sure,the supplemental statement attached to the i form does not go on to reiterate these factual details,although it does repeat or elaborate on certain events that were described in the i form.by the same token,the supplemental statement describes several additional episodes not included in the first instance in the i form limited space.fairly viewed on this record,however,any differences among the two documents appear to be elaborations,not internal inconsistencies.thus,contrary to the ij and bia characterization,the record reflects that,beginning with his very first filing,mboowa consistently alleged his hip was injured and his cousin was beheaded.these purported omissions are not actually present in the record.jin lin,at.
