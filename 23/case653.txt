iii.discussion 
california mechanic lien law used to occupy sections through of the civil code now it occupies sections through.the change was effective july.the legislature specifically provided that the old law would govern for mechanic liens recorded before that date.see.so when we cite or discuss a mechanic lien statute in the of the civil code,it is old law.we will dispense with the repetitive adjective former in front of those citations or references.
a.did cass forfeit its mechanic lien as applied to kohl,by failing to mention kohl in its preliminary notice no.
kohl makes an in passim argument in its brief to the effect that because cass knew the project would involve kohl,it was therefore required to name kohl in its preliminary notice.this is easily the weakest of all of kohl arguments.the text of the preliminary notice statute,section,only required the preliminary notice to be served on the owner or reputed owner.kohl fit neither description at the time of the preliminary notice.cass filed its preliminary notice on march.kohl acquired title on may.section did not require inclusion of likely future owners,and the phrase reputed owner hardly encompasses mere possible future owners.
was cass mechanic lien void because cass performed work after recording the lien no.
kohl argues that because cass own records showed some work performed after january,the date of its recording its lien,the lien was premature and void.cass responds that this work was punch list work.the dictionary definition of punch list is a list of small repairs or unfinished work that must be completed to fulfill a contract 
but the issue is a little more complicated than the parties present it.first,the statute governing the first date to record a mechanic lien was section.the key phrase in that statute was after he completes his contract.the lead case interpreting that phrase in section is howard wright const.bbic investors,llc th,which reversed a trial court judgment dismissing a lien foreclosure action.the trial court had based its conclusion on the rationale that the subcontractor lien was premature and therefore void.that was error on the trial court part.but the reason the trial court was wrong was not because work done after recording a lien is irrelevant.rather,the appellate court said the lien was still good because the general contractor had breached the contract on the day before the subcontractor filed its mechanic lien.see id.at because of general contractor anticipatory breach of contract,subcontractor completed its contract within meaning of section on june,so the recording of its claim of lien on june was not premature.
in reversing,the howard wright court set down two statements of law.alas those two statements are in tension with each other.first,the howard wright court articulated a substantial completion rule,saying,a contractor completes the contract upon substantial performance of its obligations.howard wright,supra,th at.but then the court turned around and used the categorical word all in interpreting section simply put,a contract is complete for purposes of commencing the recordation period under section when all work under the contract has been performed,excused,or otherwise discharged.howard wright,supra,th at.
we need not resolve the tension between substantial and all in this case,however.there was evidence from cass president,kyle nelson,that and cass agreed to terminate cass remaining work in january.cass gets the benefit of the doubt on the difference between mid and late,so we may draw the reasonable inference from that testimony that the contract had been breached prior to the recording of the lien on january.given that inference,the case comes squarely within the rule of decision of howard wright the contract was over by january,when cass filed its lien,ergo the lien was not premature and not void.
c.did cass and forfeit their mechanic liens as applied to kohl by failing to name kohl in their actual,recorded liens no.
neither cass nor named kohl as an owner in their liens,despite kohl ownership of parcel within the tract by the time those liens were recorded.accordingly kohl argues the liens are void because of the omission.
the statute setting out the requirements for a mechanic lien was section,which was essentially definitional.all it did was explain what a mechanic lien is.as with the preliminary notice statute,the key phrase was owner or reputed owner,if known.
the phrase if known is obviously a different concept from if know able.thus grinnell fire protection system american sav.loan d squarely held that title searches are not necessary for a valid mechanic lien furthermore,as the general contractor points out,other sections of the mechanic lien law contain language indicating that the legislature did not intend to impose on the lien claimant the burden and expense of conducting a title search on the property against which the lien is filed.thus,section provides that a claim of lien must contain the name of the owner or reputed owner,if known,and section similarly requires service of the preliminary day notice on the owner or reputed owner.it appears by the language used in these sections that the legislature recognized that the lien claimant might not have complete and accurate information on the ownership of the property in question,and this recognition makes it improbable that the legislature expected the materialman to conduct a complete and accurate title search to ascertain who owns the property.grinnell fire,supra,d at,italics added.
the question still remains whether cass or actually knew that kohl was an owner of a parcel within the tract when they filed their mechanic liens,since the statutory phrase if known suggests at least a requirement of listing those owners a lien claimant actually knows to be owners.however,that is a substantial evidence question on which the trial court impliedly found against kohl.and there was indeed substantial evidence to support the trial court finding neither cass nor knew kohl was an owner.as to cass,its president kyle nelson testified he did know kohl was an owner when cass recorded its lien.the trial court believed him.that was enough,major western home ins.th,the testimony of a single credible witness even if a party to the action may constitute substantial evidence.ditto as to.its accounts receivable and office manager,linda goral,similarly testified that did know of any owner of the tract other than inland prior to august.mechanic lien was filed in june.
was not listing the right address for the kohl store fatal to its mechanic lien as to kohl no.
section quoted in footnote above merely required that a mechanic lien provide a description of the property sufficient for identification.here,kohl argues that because specified second street in its lien,when the address of the kohl store on parcel of the tract is second street,that its mechanic lien is fatally defective.
as it turns out,the address is physically a little closer to the address than one might guess.the address is in the upper northwest corner of parcel,and readers will recall that parcel is immediately beside the larger parcel owned by kohl.parcel actually has no building on it,but does have some curb and gutter work by.even so,there is no question that is an address that is not actually on parcel of the tract.
according to kohl,by listing an address that did not include parcel the only property actually owned by kohl mechanic lien necessarily excluded parcel from its ambit.the theory is a variation of the linguistic canon of expressio unius exclusio alterius,and a few old cases relied on by kohl,particularly fernandez burleson cal.and nofziger lumber waters,used that linguistic principle to conclude that the property descriptions in those cases were fatal to the liens involved.see fernandez,supra,cal.at the monuments and lines by which the property is said in the notice to be particularly described can not be expunged from the notice,but must be read as part of it so read it is misleading in a particular where it should be substantially true nofziger,supra,indeed,the accurate and certain description in the notice of lien upon lot in block necessarily precludes the idea that such description could have reference to or afford any means for identifying property in some other block in said tract.
but these old cases do not give an accurate picture of the current state of the law bearing on whether a mechanic lien sufficiently identifies the property.the issue of the accuracy of a property description has been problematic in california mechanic lien case law for more than years,and the results have not always been consistent.see wand san gabriel valley lbr.d,reconciliation of all decisions in this field is perhaps impossible 
many of the early cases involved liens against mining claims.a very early case,penrose calkins cal,illustrates what is probably the low point in absolutely insufficient descriptions.there,because a scrivener apparently forgot the balance of the legal description,the mechanic lien literally only specified a certain lot and parcel in nevada county,and then forgot to describe that lot and parcel.ibid.obviously just saying the liened land is somewhere in a given county is not sufficient.
the two old cases on which kohl relies,namely nofziger and fernandez,however,are not good law because of an intervening statute,section,whose predecessor was first enacted in as section of the code of civil procedure section provided in pertinent part no mistake or errors in the the description of the property against which the lien is recorded,shall invalidate the lien,unless the court finds that such mistake or error in the statement of the demand,credits and offsets,or of the balance due,was made with the intent to defraud,or that an innocent third party,without notice,direct or constructive,has since the claim was recorded become the bona fide owner of the property,and that the notice of claim was so deficient that it did not put the party on further inquiry in any manner.italics added.
because section predecessor statute was enacted in,it obviously had no effect on the fernandez case.nor did it have any bearing on the nofziger case.nofziger came to the court based on facts occurring prior to the statute.in fact,nofziger explicitly said the case was decided on prior law.see nofziger,supra.at we forebear any discussion of the effect which section stats,might have upon the case,for the reason that all proceedings in the action were had prior to the enactment of said section.
the reliance by fernandez and nofziger on pre would be the end of the matter,except for a more recent case well,as recent as world war ii hayward.pride.d hayward lumber.hayward lumber was not decided on pre facts,but it still held a description to be fatally defective despite section predecessor being on the books at the time.in hayward lumber,the lien said section of a certain township,when the correct description was the northwest of section.id.at,italics deleted.the hayward lumber court recognized that two of the three cases it was relying on,nofziger and fernandez,did not have the benefit of the statute at that time,still section of the code of civil procedure.but it noted that there was a case decided long after after the enactment of the statute,bishop hayward lumber investment d bishop,which also held a description to be insufficient.based primarily on the bishop case,the hayward lumber case held the description before it to be fatally defective.hayward lumber,supra,d at.
we decline to follow hayward lumber because we conclude hayward lumber misapplied bishop.bishop is a case analogous to a situation where your neighbors hire a contractor,do pay the contractor,the contractor then files a mechanic lien giving the address of your house,and the court holds quite sensibly that the contractor does get to foreclose on your house because,after all,the contractor listed the wrong property.that obviously a situation where section should apply anyway.section was never intended to allow liens on property not benefitted by the work or materials represented by the lien.in our analogy,though,the contractor still might have a valid lien against your neighbor property,and in point of fact the bishop court said exactly that 
the cases that are good precedent on the issue before us include bothum kreis,american transit mix weber d,and borello eichler homes,d.as in the case before us,the lien claimant in bothumjust plain got the address wrong folsom street in los angeles when the right address was folsom street,los angeles bothum,supra.at.applying the predecessor statute to section,the court excused the error,noting that on the record before the court one could say the erroneous street number actually designated any actual street number or building on folsom street.id.at.
in american transit,the lien claimant made a error,lot in block of richland tract,when the right description was lot in block of richland tract.american transit,supra,d at.the court summarily dismissed the argument the mistake was fatal merely by referring to the statute then in its section incarnation and paraphrasing how it worked.the implication was that if there were no fraud or prejudice to a later buyer,nothing more need be said.see id.at.
in borello,the lien claimant said unit and unit,terra linda,san raphael when he should have said terra linda valley.see borello,supra,d at,italics added.terra linda valley was,in fact,a separate subdivision,as were two others that had the name terra linda in them.id.at.indeed,the general area was known as terra linda.the court reasoned that the lien was good because it gave a general geographical area that embraced one of four subdivisions,and there were other identifying facts,most importantly that the owner never owned any unit or in terra linda valley.see id.at.
there is no hint in this case that had any intent to defraud,or that an innocent party buying from kohl would not be on notice of the lien.kohl argument that listing of an address that did not apply to kohl property necessarily precludes application to kohl property as a matter of law therefore fails.

e.did the statute of limitations for filing a foreclosure action run on either cass or foreclosure actions no.
the basic facts bearing on the question of whether the statute of limitations ran on either cass or mechanic lien are not in dispute section provided for a day statute of limitations to file a foreclosure action,which begins to run from the filing of the lien.both cass and met that day deadline when they filed an action the key phrase from the statute itself to foreclose their liens,but in neither case did they name either kohl or wells fargo in their original complaints.and both kohl and wells fargo were owners of parcels in the tract at the time of the original complaints.only later,by the device of substituting kohl or wells fargo in place of various does in their original complaints,were cass and able to obtain judgments foreclosing their liens that applied to kohl and wells fargo interests in the tract.
the actual language of the day statute of limitations applying to mechanic liens only requires an action be filed within the days.see grinnell fire,supra,d at,recognizing point made by appellant.beginning in,however,in paramount sec.daze,the court of appeal engrafted a common law requirement that the mechanic lien foreclosure action must be brought against all of those whose rights,estates,or interests are claimed to be adverse and subordinate.id.at,quoting continental commercial trust sav.bank pacific coast pipe cir,construing analogous idaho statute.the theory of the paramount court was that parties having an interest in the property had the basic right to contest either the validity or priority of the asserted mechanic lien.see id.at.that theory was amplified by the supreme court in connolly development,superior court cal d,where the court observed that mechanic liens do subject owners to the deprivation of their property,and thus constitute a taking of property by the government.accordingly,said connolly,there must be due process protections before property owners can lose their land to mechanic lien foreclosures.see id.at.the owner whose property has been subjected to a mechanics lien can not be denied the protection of the due process clause.
a decade after connolly,the appellate court in grinnell fire dealt with the question of whether doe pleading might solve the problem of a lien claimant who is actually certain of who is the owner of the property being liened.grinnell fire reasoned that since it was clear the legislature never intended to impose on mechanic lien claimants the expense of title searches,the requirement that all interested parties must be named in the foreclosure complaint could be satisfied by the device of doe pleading pursuant to section of the code of civil procedure.see grinnell fire,supra,d at.but as a caveat,grinnell fire recognized that doe pleadings are only allowed when the plaintiff does not have actual knowledge of the identity of the party latter named as an initial doe defendant.see id.at.
by way of illustration of the power of doe pleading as approved in grinnell fire,the court in westfour california first bank th held that the statute of limitations did not run in a case where the lien claimant only knew a bank was in the probable position of being a construction lender,but still did not have actual knowledge of that bank deed of trust against the property at the time it filed its original complaint.see id.at,italics added.
given this history,the statute of limitations issue before us boils down,as both wells fargo and kohl acknowledge,to whether cass or had actual knowledge of either wells fargo or kohl interests in the property at the time they filed their original complaints to foreclose the tract both wells fargo and kohl assert there was actual knowledge on the part of both cass and,though they arrive there by slightly different routes.
we begin with wells fargo,as successor to wachovia.wells fargo,obviously laboring in the shadow of westfour,tries to bridge the crevice between a only being known to probably have had a deed of trust on some property and b being known to actually have had one,by reference to a definitional statute,section.section stated that the definition of a construction lender for purposes of the mechanic lien law was an entity that is a beneficiary of a deed of trust in the property 
the argument,however,fails because doe pleading depends on the actual and subjective knowledge of lien claimant,while section,at most,gives a legal definition of construction lender and hence only constructive knowledge.constructive knowledge is a fiction.it imputed knowledge,not actual knowledge.thus it does not follow from the mere existence of a deed of trust on a given property that a lien claimant actually knows there a construction lender,or who that construction lender is.
the proof that only actual,and not constructive,knowledge will defeat a doe pleading is found in a case relied on by the grinnell fire court,irving carpentier cal.there,the plaintiff sued to quiet title in certain land in alameda county.he named a number of defendants including leland stanford and charles crocker,but failed to name a particular defendant even though he would have learned of the defendant interest if he had merely looked in a book of records.id.at.but such constructive knowledge did not,said our high court,prevent him from using the device of doe pleadings under section.the court drew a clear line between a actual ignorance and b what he might have learned had he conducted a title search moreover,the affidavit of douty does not show that plaintiff was ignorant.it shows that if he had examined a book of records named he would have discovered that a certain deed purported to be there recorded.but this does not show that the plaintiff was ignorant of the fact when he commenced his suit.it merely shows that he failed to examine a certain book,which if he had examined would have afforded him some information.his failure to examine tends to establish the fact of ignorance,italics added.since wells fargo does point to any uncontroverted evidence showing cass actually knew wells fargo or wachovia had a deed of trust on the property when it filed its suit,its statute of limitations argument fails.
kohl argument is a little different it a substantial evidence challenge.kohl points to evidence that cass contacted kohl in relation to obtaining payment from.such evidence means,says kohl,cass necessarily knew and this is where kohl brief uses some conspicuously circumlocutory language of kohl identity and relationship to cass claims.kohl ca quite bring itself to state outright that cass knew kohl was an owner.kohl must fall back on whatever insinuation can be eked out of the fact that people knew kohl had an involvement in the construction of one of its stores.

did the court err no.three statutes,sections,and,when read together,are incompatible with kohl and wells fargo proposed per se rule that a judgment foreclosing a mechanic lien can never exceed the amount stated in the lien itself.first,section quoted in footnote above merely requires mechanic liens to state a demand.the word demand does not denote exactitude.rather it denotes a claim which is subject to either negotiation or litigation.see root american equity specialty ins.th,the action or fact of demanding or claiming in legal form a legal claim to ask for a thing with legal right or authority to claim as something one is legally or rightfully entitled to.quoting oxford english dict.ed.the use of the word demand suggests that the fit between the amount listed in the lien and the amount given by a foreclosure judgment need not be exact.
second,section provided for a percent forfeiture of a mechanic lien if the lien willfully includes amounts of work,labor and material not actually furnished the provision obviously operated to give lien claimants a major incentive not to assert any items which can not be supported in the of lien foreclosure litigation.but there is a necessary logical implication to section harshness the legislature was clearly giving lien claimants an incentive to err,if at all,on the low side in stating lien claims,with the implication being an opportunity to make up for it in the actual foreclosure judgment.

kohl and wells fargo make no argument that the amount of the judgment is based on a figure beyond the contract price or that the amount was somehow unreasonable.kohl and wells fargo argument,rather,is based on the analogy of mechanic liens to mortgages the foreclosure of a mechanic lien resembles the foreclosure of a mortgage,and when it comes to mortgages there is no question the recovery must be limited to the amount secured by the mortgage,ergo the recovery on the foreclosure of a mechanic lien is also limited by the stated amount of the lien.
but kohl and wells fargo read too much into their analogy.while both areas of law involve foreclosure,the origins of the right to foreclose are decidedly different.the foreclosure of a mortgages ultimately is a matter of voluntary contract the foreclosure of a mechanic lien at least in a case involving an innocent owner is a matter of the imposition of an obligation on a landowner independent of contract 
the case on which kohl and wells fargo rely for the mechanic analogy,laubisch roberdo cal d,merely stands for the proposition that the purchaser at a mechanic lien foreclosure sale is just as much the new owner of the property as the purchaser at a mortgage foreclosure sale.in laubisch,there was a judgment ordering a mechanic lien foreclosure in january but there was no actual foreclosure sale until march.world war ii intervened,and,during the war,the owner of the property at the time of the mechanic lien transferred the property to a woman named roberdo,who in turn transferred the property to a woman named wentworth,who in turn transferred it to a woman named cowan,and when the purchaser at the mechanic lien foreclosure sought to quiet title and obtain rent from cowan,cowan argued she had acquired the property by adverse possession.in the process of knocking down cowan adverse possession claim,the supreme court simply made the point that no statute of limitations was running against the purchaser at the mechanic lien foreclosure,just as no statute of limitations would have run against a purchaser at a mortgage foreclosure sale.id.at.such a rationale hardly stands for the proposition that the judgment foreclosing the mechanic lien is per se limited in amount to the amount stated in the lien

j.did the trial court err in awarding the lien claimants prejudgment interest at percent,as distinct from percent,as against the non contracting,innocent owners yes.
this is the only part of this opinion certified for publication,so we set forth a brief prcis of the relevant facts for the benefit of those readers who will not have access to the balance of the opinion in,a developer named inland,engaged a general contractor called,to develop a kohl department store and surrounding property on a tract in beaumont.the construction lender was wachovia bank.general contractor contracted with,among others,palomar grading and cass to do infrastructural work benefitting the tract.as it turned out,both kohl and wachovia ended up owning parcels in the tract.neither,however,ever entered a contract with the two subcontractors.palomar grading and cass were not paid for substantial portions of their work,and brought successful actions to foreclose their mechanic liens the trial judge awarded them prejudgment interest at percent and now kohl and wells fargo,as wachovia successor,are challenging that decision.we write on a clean slate.no published case has considered the issue 
there is a default rate of interest prescribed by the constitution in article xv,section,for the forbearance of any money,goods,or things in action,or on accounts after demand,shall be percent per annum.italics added.things in action obviously include the right to foreclose on a mechanic lien.the legislature,however,has enacted section,subdivision b,specifying that the default for breach of contract is percent the lien claimants in this case argue for the applicability of section percent,as distinct from the constitutional default rate of percent.
we determine it is the constitutional default rate that should apply to prejudgment interest on a mechanic lien as applied to noncontracting,innocent owners.unlike section,the prejudgment interest statute,section,applies to both contract and tort actions.see marine terminals paceco,d.torts generally do not involve obligations incurred by contract.
the salient fact here is that the owners of the property had no contract with either palomar grading or cass.the liens on the property are the result of the imposition of the mechanic lien laws,not contract.the comments of an appellate panel from the depths of the great depression summarize the essence of the legal relationship the right of a materialman to have declared and enforced his lien against specific property is a right provided to him by the organic law,and shall not be lightly considered.it is true perhaps,that changing conditions have,in some cases,permitted an apparent injustice to result through enforcement of liens against an innocent owner,or one defrauded through the machinations of a dishonest contractor.bay lumber pickering,italics added.
we must not forget that,as explained by our supreme court in connolly development,superior court cal d,the operation of mechanic liens do constitute a taking of an owner interest in its property the owner whose property has been subjected to a mechanic lien has suffered at least as serious a deprivation as did the plaintiffs in two united state supreme court cases,and thus can not be denied the protection of the due process clause.id.at.but the taking is justified,in terms of an owner who is innocent of breaching a contract with an unpaid lien claimant,precisely because the owner property has been benefitted by the work or material of the lien claimant.the purpose of a mechanics lien is to prevent unjust enrichment of a property owner at the expense of laborers or material suppliers.basic modular facilities,ehsanipour th.but that benefit is not necessarily the result of an owner contract with the lien claimant.just as prejudgment interest can be given in tort cases,an owner who did contract with a lien claimant can be legally exposed to prejudgment interest.it follows,then,that the statutory default rate for contract breaches of percent should not be applicable to innocent,noncontracting owners,who are analogous to tort defendants,not contract defendants.
the lien claimants here posit two theories against our conclusion.palomar grading is direct.it simply is fair,says palomar grading,to charge percent against the improver of land by which it appears palomar grading means a general contractor who breaches a contract with a subcontractor and let an owner get away with only percent.but it is fair,when one realizes the owner loss is a matter of operation of law,not voluntary choice.unfortunately,general contractors sometimes stiff their subcontractors after taking money from the owner or the owner banker.in that situation the mechanic lien law in effect forces innocent owners to pay twice.to be sure,their paying twice may be justified by the special solicitude shown toward unpaid contractors and material suppliers by the mechanic lien law that law is,after all,part of the california constitution article xx,section but the justification for the loss of property in the case of innocent owners is certainly not that they agreed to it.thus it is perfectly fair that the defaulting contractor who breached its contract should have to pay a higher rate than an innocent owner who did.
cass makes a different argument.it focuses on its right to recover the amount due according to its contract.for cass,because it is entitled to recover the amount due pursuant to its contract it should receive percent interest,period.but that logic ignores section.the statutory language is the liens provided for in this chapter shall be direct liens,and shall be for the reasonable value of the labor,services,equipment,or materials furnished or for the price agreed upon by the claimant and the person with whom he or she contracted,whichever is less.italics added.thus a lien claimant does not necessarily receive the amount due on its contract.again,we see that insofar as the mechanic lien laws act on an innocent owner,the obligation is limited by statute to the reasonable value of work when it is less than the contract.
cass brief also cites some language from a comment apparently in the edition of the respected marsh treatise on mechanic lien law,to the effect it is safe to say only that the lien claimant right to interest is limited to the legal rate now.there are two answers to the proffered quotation one,the quotation only refers to a limit,not what is actually appropriate,and does at least in the snippet cass gives us address prejudgment interest as such.and two,while the safe to say language might have been found in the edition of marsh,it is not to be found in section of either the hard copy of current edition or the current online version.see marsh,cal.mechanics lien law ed,to.moreover,that particular section of marsh is written as a practice guide from the point of view of an attorney for a lien claimant,and the thrust of the section is not this is the law,but there no harm in trying.
obviously our brief decision in regard to the prejudgment interest issue does not address the question of prejudgment interest in the case of culpable,owners,that is,owners who breach their contracts with contractors or materials suppliers and for that reason have a mechanic lien slapped on their property.we also do not address the possibility to be sure of a sham change in ownership from say,a breaching owner to an ostensibly one done deliberately to try to gain a better interest rate.our decision today only applies to innocent owners who,even though they did not breach their own contracts,nonetheless winded up with property subject to a mechanic lien.
