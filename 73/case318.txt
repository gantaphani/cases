definition of a capital asset 
a capital gain or loss is created by the sale or exchange of a capital asset held for more than year.section of the internal revenue code defines a capital asset as property held by the taxpayer whether or not connected with his trade or business.this provision excludes from the definition certain property categories,none of which is applicable here 
a supreme court decision suggested that this definition can be construed too broadly,stating that it is evident that not everything which can be called property in the ordinary sense and which is outside the statutory exclusions qualifies as a capital asset.comm r gillette motor,d.the court noted that it had long held that the term capital asset is to be construed narrowly in accordance with the purpose of congress to afford treatment only in situations typically involving the realization of appreciation in value accrued over a substantial period of time,and thus to ameliorate the hardship of taxation of the entire gain in one year.id.but the supreme court decision in arkansas best commissioner,d,at least at first blush,seems to have reversed that narrow reading.arkansas best suggests instead that the definition is to be broadly construed.see id.at.the body of establishes a general definition of the term capital asset,and the phrase does not include takes out of that broad definition only the classes of property that are specifically mentioned.
b.the doctrine 
the problem with an overly broad definition for capital assets is that it could encompass some things congress did not intend to be taxed as capital gains.maginnis,at.an overly broad definition,linked with favorable tax treatment,would encourage transactions designed to convert ordinary income into capital gains.see id.at.for example,a salary is taxed as ordinary income,and the right to be paid for work is a person property.but it is hard to conceive that congress intends for taxpayers to get treatment if they were to sell their rights,property held by the taxpayer to their future paychecks.see boris bittker lawrence lokken,federal taxation of income,estates and gifts ed.
to get around this problem,courts have created the doctrine.this doctrine says,in effect,that lump sum consideration that seems essentially a substitute for what would otherwise be received at a future time as ordinary income may not be taxed as a capital gain.maginnis,at quoting comm r lake,d alteration in original.
the seminal case is the supreme court decision in hort commissioner.hort had inherited a building from his father,and one of the building tenants canceled its lease,paying hort a cancellation fee of.id.at.hort argued that the cancellation fee was capital gain,but the court disagreed,holding that the cancellation fee was ordinary income because the cancellation of the lease involved nothing more than relinquishment of the right to future rental payments in return for a present substitute payment and possession of the leased premises.id.at.
the supreme court bolstered the doctrine in lake.lake,was an oil and company with a working interest in two oil and gas leases.at.it assigned an oil payment right payable out of percent of the oil attributable to lake working interest in the two leases.id.at.lake reported this assignment as a sale of property taxable as capital gain.id.but the court disagreed,holding that the consideration received was taxable as ordinary income.id.at.the court reasoning gave full voice to the doctrine the lump sum consideration seems essentially a substitute for what would otherwise be received at a future time as ordinary income.id.at.
our court has rarely dealt with this doctrine.we have only cited lake in,tunnell united states,cir,and once in,hempt,united states,cir citing lake with approval,but deciding the case under a of transfers of property for corporate.
the latteras argue that the doctrine,which takes property held by the taxpayer outside the statutory definition,did not survive arkansas best.but although arkansas best ostensibly cabined the exceptions to the statutory definition,it made clear that the line of cases,based on the premise that property does not include claims or rights to ordinary income,ha d no application in the present context.arkansas best,at.the tax court has several times confirmed that arkansas best in no way affected the viability of the principle established in the line of cases.davis,at citing cases.and the ninth circuit agrees.maginnis,at.we follow suit,holding that the doctrine remains viable in the wake of arkansas best.
but there is a tension in the doctrine in theory,all capital assets are substitutes for ordinary income.see,william klein et,federal income taxation ed.a fundamental principle of economics is that the value of an asset is equal to the present discounted value of all the expected net receipts from that asset over its life.see also lake,at.noting that the to be ordinary for an asset was the present value of income which the recipient would otherwise obtain in the future.for example,a stock value is the present discounted value of the company future profits.see,maginnis,at united states dresser,cir applying this concept to the value of land.r ead literally,the doctrine would completely swallow the concept of capital gains.levine,supra,at accord bittker lokken,supra,at unless restrained,the theory thus threatens even the most familiar capital gain transactions.also,an overbroad substitute for ordinary income doctrine,besides being analytically unsatisfactory,would create the potential for the abuse of treating capital losses as ordinary.levine,supra,at.the doctrine must therefore be limited so as not to err on either side.

character of the asset 
the fifth circuit in dresser industries noted that t here is,in law and fact,a vast difference between the present sale of the future right to earn income and the present sale of the future right to earned income.dresser,at emphasis in original.the taxpayer in dresser industries had assigned its right to an exclusive patent license back to the patent holder in exchange for a share of the licensing fees from licensees.id.at.the court used this right to earn income right to earned income distinction to hold that treatment was applicable.it noted that the asset sold was not a right to earned income,to be paid in the future,but was a property which would produce income.id.at.further,it disregarded the ordinary nature of the income generated by the asset because all property produces ordinary income,the sale of such property does not result in treatment.id.this can be seen in the sale of bonds,which produce ordinary income,but the sale of which is treated as capital gain.
sinclair explains the distinction in this way earned income conveys the concept that the income has already been earned and the holder of the right to this income only has to collect it.in other words,the owner of the right to earned income is entitled to the income merely by virtue of owning the property.sinclair,supra,at.he gives as examples of this concept rental income,stock dividends,and rights to future lottery payments.id.of course,in the wake of dividend tax reform,stock dividends are now taxed as capital gains.h.for the right to earn income,on the other hand,the holder of such right must do something further to earn the income because mere ownership of the right to earn income does not entitle the owner to income.sinclair,supra,at.following dresser industries,sinclair gives a patent as an example of this concept.id.
assets that constitute a right to earn income merit treatment,while those that are a right to earned income merit treatment.our court implicitly made this distinction in tunnell united states,cir.tunnell withdrew from a law partnership,and he assigned his rights in the law firm in exchange for.id.at.when he withdrew,the partnership had over in uncollected accounts receivable from work that had already been done.id.we agreed with the district court that the sale of a partnership is treated as the sale of a capital asset.id.the sale of a partnership does not,in and of itself,confer income on the buyer the buyer must continue to provide legal services,so it is a sale of the right to earn income.consequently,as we held,the sale of a partnership receives treatment.the accounts receivable,on the other hand,had already been earned the buyer of the partnership only had to remain a partner to collect that income,so the sale of accounts receivable is the sale of the right to earned income.thus,we held that the portion of the purchase price that reflected the sale of the accounts receivable was taxable as ordinary income.id.at.
similarly,when an erstwhile employee is paid a termination fee for a contract,that employee still possesses the asset the right to provide certain personal services and the money the termination fee has already been earned and will simply be paid.the employee no longer has to perform any more services in exchange for the fee,so this is not like dresser industries s right to earn income.these termination fees are therefore rights to earned income and should be treated as ordinary income.see,elliott united states,cir holt comm r,cir see also chirelstein,supra,at noting that courts have held consistently that payments made to an employee for the surrender of his employment contract are ordinary.
